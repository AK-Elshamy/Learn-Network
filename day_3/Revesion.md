- ## **Subnetting and Network Segmentation:**

  - A larger network is divided into several smaller groups called **subnets**.
  - Communication **within** these smaller networks (subnets) is facilitated by the subnet structure.

- **Logical Address (IP Address):**

  - The **Logical Address** is also known as the **IP (Internet Protocol) Address**.
  - It is responsible for **connecting and routing data** across different networks.

- **Routing and Routers:**

  - Data routing is primarily done through a **router**.
  - The **router** connects these small groups (subnets/smaller networks) to each other, enabling communication between them.

- **Physical Address (MAC Address) and Switches:**
  - A **switch** is used to manage communication _within_ a local network segment (a subnet).
  - A switch operates using the **MAC (Media Access Control) Address** to communicate and direct data frames to the correct device locally.
